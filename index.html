The Sonadia Trip Experience

Our much-anticipated trip to Sonadia Island began on the night of December 27, 2024, at 9 PM. Excitement filled the air as we boarded our transport and set off into the night. After hours of traveling under the starry sky, we finally arrived at Kolatoli Point in Cox's Bazar at 5:30 AM on December 28, 2024. The early morning serenity was enchanting, and the fresh sea breeze invigorated us..

The Journey to Sonadia Begins

We took some time to have a hearty breakfast, which gave us the energy needed for the adventurous day ahead. By 6:30 AM, we rented jeeps to take us to the jetty point, our departure spot for the boat ride to Sonadia. The jeep ride, though bumpy, added to the thrill as we sped past scenic coastal views. By 7:00 AM, we reached the jetty point, where the boats awaited us. The real adventure began at 7:30 AM as we boarded the boat and embarked on our journey to the breathtaking Sonadia Island. The boat ride was a serene experience; the cool breeze and gentle waves set the perfect tone for what was to come. Finally, at 9:00 AM, the boat anchored, and we stepped onto the shores of Sonadia.

Exploring Sonadia Island

Upon arrival, we walked down to the beach, soaking in the untouched beauty of the island. As we settled in, a delightful breakfast of dim khichuri (egg fried rice and lentils) awaited us. The meal, simple yet flavorful, gave us the perfect start to the day. After breakfast, we headed to the pristine Sonadia Beach, where we spent hours immersed in fun and relaxation. From playing games and splashing in the crystal-clear waters to simply lying on the soft sands and soaking up the sun, we made the most of the breathtaking surroundings. The natural beauty of Sonadia, with its serene beaches and untouched wilderness, made every moment magical.

A Memorable Lunch

By 2 PM, it was time for lunch, and we were fortunate to be hosted by a kind and hospitable resident of Sonadia. The meal they prepared was a true taste of the island’s local flavors—fresh sea fish, alu vorta (mashed potatoes), daal (lentils), and vaat (rice). It was a feast that reflected the simplicity and warmth of island life, and we couldn’t have been more grateful for the generosity shown to us.

The Return Journey and Unexpected Challenges

After finishing lunch, we returned to our tents for a short rest. A quick 10 to 20 minutes was all we could afford before we began packing our belongings. By 5:30 PM, we were ready to leave. However, nature had its own plans for us. The ebb tide had set in, making it impossible for the boats to come close to the shore. This left us with no choice but to walk through the muddy coast, waist-deep in water, to reach the boats. While challenging, it was an experience that added a sense of adventure to our return. The boat finally started its journey back to Cox’s Bazar at 7 PM, gliding through the calm evening waters. After two hours, we reached Cox’s Bazar at 9 PM.

Evening at Cox’s Bazar

Once back on land, we took some time for ourselves to freshen up and enjoy the ambiance of Cox’s Bazar. Dinner was wrapped up by 10:30 PM, and we spent the remaining time walking along the iconic Cox’s Bazar sea beach. The moonlit night, the sound of the waves crashing against the shore, and the cool breeze made for a perfect ending to the day.

The Journey Back to Dhaka

At 12 AM, we gathered to board the bus. By 12:30 AM, we were ready, and the bus set off at 1:30 AM on December 29, 2024, marking the beginning of our journey back to Dhaka. Exhausted but content, most of us slept through the ride. The bus reached Dhaka at 8:30 AM, and by 11 AM, we were finally home, bringing the unforgettable adventure to an end.

Final Thoughts

The Sonadia trip was more than just a journey; it was an experience that brought us closer to nature and to each other. From the stunning beaches to the warm hospitality of the locals, every moment was filled with joy and discovery. It’s a trip that will forever hold a special place in our hearts.
